Meet Jeremy Marcus

Jeremy Marcus was born and raised in New Rochelle, New York, in a household defined by public service and strong community values. His mother is a public school teacher, his father works in public relations, and his younger sister — still in high school — keeps him grounded in what really matters.

A graduate of New Rochelle High School and longtime member of Beth El Synagogue, Jeremy grew up believing that public service isn’t about slogans — it’s about results. He studied political science and economics at Binghamton University, where he led civic initiatives and fought for progressive, practical reforms.

Jeremy interned for New York State Senator Lea Webb in Binghamton, helping push forward legislation on healthcare, education, and climate justice. He also wrote on public policy and governance for the university’s paper, BU Pipe Dream, consistently promoting bold but grounded ideas to make government work better for everyone.

Now, as a Democrat running for President, Jeremy is ready to bring that energy, humility, and real-world experience to the highest office in the land — to rebuild America with integrity, accountability, and action from Day One.
